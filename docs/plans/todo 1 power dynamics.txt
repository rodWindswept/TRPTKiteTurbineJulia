Analyzed

force_analysis.jl#L62-110
There it is — the problem is clear. Here's the breakdown:

The bug: Aero thrust doesn't depend on ω
Line 65:

julia
P_aero = 0.5 * p.rho * v_hub^3 * π * p.rotor_radius^2 * p.cp * cos(p.elevation_angle)^3
This computes the same P_aero regardless of rotor speed. Then:

julia
F_thrust = P_aero / v_eff   # line 85 — also independent of ω
T_aero   = F_thrust / p.n_lines
So the aerodynamic contribution to tether tension doesn't change at all between 7 rpm and 70 rpm. The only ω-dependent term is centrifugal (line 94), but tether segment mass is tiny (~0.07 kg with Dyneema density), so m_seg × ω² × r_i adds negligibly even at 70 rpm.

Why this is physically wrong
In reality, a rotor at 70 rpm (tip speed ratio λ = ωR/v ≈ 3.3) vs 7 rpm (λ ≈ 0.33) experiences:

Much higher blade apparent wind — the tangential velocity ωR dominates at high TSR, so total apparent wind ≈ √(v² + (ωR)²) is much larger
Higher thrust coefficient CT(λ) — thrust increases with TSR (CT is typically 0.8–0.9 near optimal TSR, vs near-zero at very low TSR)
The relationship P = T × v only holds for an ideal actuator disc — real rotors at high TSR have CT/CP ratios of 4–5×, meaning much more thrust than you'd infer from power alone
The current model fundamentally lacks:

A CT(λ) thrust curve — thrust as a function of tip speed ratio
A Cp(λ) power curve — power coefficient as a function of TSR (currently constant at 0.22)
Both of these are essential for realistic tension predictions. The 

Rotor_TRTP_Sizing_Iteration2.xlsx
 AeroDyn BEM data should contain exactly these curves.

What the fix looks like
The proper fix is two-fold:

Power: Replace Cp = 0.22 (constant) with Cp(λ) — a lookup table or polynomial fit from the AeroDyn BEM data. At low λ (stall), Cp drops. At high λ (freewheeling), Cp drops. Peak near λ ≈ 4.1.

Thrust: Replace F_thrust = P_aero / v_eff with:

F_thrust = 0.5 × ρ × v_hub² × π × R² × CT(λ) × cos²(β)
where CT(λ) is a separate curve from the BEM data. This makes thrust strongly ω-dependent.
